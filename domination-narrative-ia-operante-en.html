GhostOps intervention field

Narrative dominance & operational AI

In sensitive moments, it is not only decisions that matter, but the way they are told, repeated, distorted or appropriated. A dominant narrative always ends up structuring perception: it explains who is legitimate, who is “right”, who embodies the future or the problem.

GhostOps narrative dominance is about architecting this master storyline, then deploying it as concrete sequences: public statements, visible acts, weak signals. Operational AI is used as a tactical tool: listening, generation, testing angles, simulating reactions, measuring impact, in order to adjust sequences in real time rather than steering by gut feeling.

In 30 seconds

What it is

An intervention to regain control of the narrative around a leader, a project or a group: identify existing narratives, build an ownable master line, orchestrate sequences of communication and acts, supported by AI for testing and measurement.

Who it is for

Leaders, ExComs, boards, investment funds and communications departments faced with polarised environments, perception crises or misunderstood transformations.

Expected outcome

A clear, embodied master narrative, adapted per audience, and an AI device that allows you to verify that this narrative actually lands with your publics, instead of remaining internal storytelling only.

When to activate a narrative dominance operation?

Typical situations

  • Strategic transformation misunderstood or fought internally.
  • Leader exposed to attacks (media, networks, internal actors) without a clear response line.
  • Crises where several competing narratives clash without any one of them prevailing.
  • Need to durably reposition a group or BU in a saturated sectoral ecosystem.

Risk signals

  • Gap between the official internal narrative and what teams say “off the record”.
  • Analysts, partners or media describing your situation in terms that you do not recognise.
  • Accumulation of micro-criticisms, posts and weak signals that converge towards the same negative reading.
  • Narrative fatigue: the same talking points endlessly repeated without producing real effects.

Four narrative dominance configurations

Case #1 – Transformation perceived as pure “cost cutting”

A group launches a deep transformation of its operating model. Internally and externally, the narrative that takes hold is: “new wave of cost cutting”, whereas the real issue is the survival of the model over 5 years. Teams disengage, key talent leave, external relays align with this reading.

GhostOps intervention: analysis of existing narratives (internal, unions, media, social networks). Construction of a legitimate master narrative (securing the future, preserving jobs, reallocating margins to innovation), declined per audience. AI-driven listening playbook: detection of what sticks, adjustment of angles and proofs. Result: the “cost-cutting” narrative remains present, but is challenged by a credible future-project narrative, strong enough to retain key profiles.

Case #2 – Leader trapped in a permanent trial of intent

A leader appointed in a tense context is immediately caught in a trial of intent: “parachuted from HQ”, “too political”, “not from the field”. Every decision is read through this lens. Their authority no longer rests on their actions, but on a self-fulfilling negative reputation.

GhostOps intervention: mapping implicit accusations and unspoken expectations. Definition of a programme of visible acts and targeted public statements, sequenced over time, supported by AI work on angles, clarity and coherence. Result: over the months, the leader’s figure is no longer defined only by the initial narrative; it is progressively re-defined by their decisions and concrete proofs of usefulness.

Case #3 – Group under attack on its “ESG” legitimacy

An industrial group presents itself as committed on environmental and social issues. NGOs and internal collectives begin to denounce “ESG-washing”: some dossiers are unexplained, inconsistencies become visible. The “false virtue” narrative gains ground, including among internal talent.

GhostOps intervention: lucid analysis of weak spots, clarification of what is truly solid and what is not yet. Construction of a narrative that does not overplay virtue, but owns trajectories, limits and trade-offs. AI playbook to test formulations, simulate critical reactions and prepare responses. Result: the group no longer presents itself as exemplary, but as engaged in a credible trajectory, consistent with its real industrial constraints.

Case #4 – BU in narrative competition with another group entity

Two BUs or two countries are vying to be the group’s “showcase”. Each produces its own narrative, its numbers, its proofs. Internally, this creates ongoing competition, friction and misunderstanding. Externally, the group’s image becomes blurred.

GhostOps intervention: clarification of what each entity really brings to the global narrative, definition of a group-level storyline that does not deny local specifics but articulates them. AI used to compare external perceptions of both narratives, identify redundancies and contradictions. Result: a group narrative that integrates, orders and prioritises local stories, instead of letting them compete permanently.

How does a Narrative dominance & operational AI intervention work?

Step 1 – Mapping existing narratives

  • Collecting internal discourse (leaders, managers, employee reps, informal networks).
  • Analysing external coverage: press, social networks, analysts, partners.
  • Identifying dominant, minority and latent narratives.
  • Distinguishing factual perception, feelings, trials of intent and fantasies.

Step 2 – Designing the master narrative

  • Clarifying the real situation: constraints, trade-offs, unspoken elements, red lines.
  • Defining the master narrative: what is owned, what is corrected, what is committed to.
  • Declining it by audience: internal, employee reps, key talent, market, ecosystem, authorities.
  • Working on tone: degree of transparency, humility, firmness, depending on the case.

Step 3 – AI playbook & diffusion sequences

  • Designing an AI playbook: prompts, angle variations, wording tests.
  • Simulating possible reactions (objections, attacks, misunderstandings).
  • Choosing formats and channels: internal notes, speeches, op-eds, posts, interviews.
  • Planning sequences: what to say, where, to whom, and in which order.

Step 4 – Measurement, adjustment & anchoring

  • Tracking reception signals: direct reactions, internal indicators, external signals.
  • Narrative adjustments: clarifications, additions, inflections.
  • Capitalisation: what works for this leader, this entity, this group.
  • Transfer of an operating mode: leaving behind a device that can be used beyond the GhostOps intervention.

Tangible deliverables

  • Map of internal and external narratives surrounding your situation.
  • Master narrative and audience-specific adaptations, ready to deploy.
  • Sets of arguments, images and proofs tailored to each target.
  • Operational AI playbook (prompts, workflows, use cases) for your teams.
  • Narrative sequencing plan: calendar, channels, spokespeople, formats.
  • Synthesis note for board / ExCom: narrative risks, levers, recommendations.

What GhostOps Narrative dominance & operational AI is not

  • A deceitful manipulation exercise disconnected from real decisions.
  • A simple communication or “personal branding” campaign.
  • A gimmicky use of AI limited to producing nicely worded posts.
  • A device that ignores legal, social or ethical constraints.
