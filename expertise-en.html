Mapping GhostOps operations

GhostOps intervention fields

GhostOps intervenes when HR crises, internal power games or configurations of invisible alliances threaten the trajectory of a company, a business unit or a leader. We do not produce abstract recommendations, but operational devices that have to hold in reality: power balances, social constraints, legal risks, pressure of time.

Nine intervention fields structure these operations: containing a crisis, reconfiguring power around key figures, managing implicit loyalties, neutralising toxic counter-powers, orchestrating internal and external narratives, installing post-crisis resilience. Each field can be activated on its own or combined with the others.

This page maps these fields. Each card opens onto a detailed operational page illustrated with use cases.

Three pillars structuring GhostOps operations

1. Stabilise & contain

Intervene where the system is about to break: HR and power lock-ins, HR & governance due diligence, neutralisation of counter-powers, post-crisis resilience. Objective: avoid escalation, document, lock in acceptable outcomes.

2. Reconfigure power

Reposition leaders, manage sensitive exits or transitions, reshape invisible alliances. Objective: evolve the power map without uncontrolled rupture.

3. Control the narrative

Structure the narratives that organise internal and external perception: AI-assisted narrative dominance, external influence scenarisation. Objective: decisions that are intelligible, assumable and sustainable over time.

The 3 GhostOps programmes

Beyond the intervention fields, GhostOps operates through three structured programmes, designed to be immediately actionable by a CEO, a board or an HRD. Each programme articulates perimeter, intervention sequence and deliverables – with an engagement level compatible with high-stakes situations.

Programme 1 – Crisis & lock-ins (30–60 days)

Short, dense intervention when the crisis is already there: site under pressure, open conflict, “legalised” sequence, internal blockage around a leader or a sensitive HR decision. Objective: regain control, avoid escalation and lock in assumable outcomes.

What for?

  • Localised HR or social crises with risk of propagation.
  • Internal lock-ins (forbidden contact with certain stakeholders, blocked circuits).
  • Sensitive decisions already in motion, but without a clear exit trajectory.

Format & deliverables

  • 48-hour footprint (interviews & targeted review of documents).
  • 30 or 60-day war-room with milestones, arbitrations and sequenced messages.
  • Actor mapping, outcome scenarios and an operational action plan.

Associated fields:  HR crises & lock-ins, Neutralisation of counter-powers, Post-crisis resilience.

Programme 2 – Governance & power (60–90 days)

Programme focused on the real power map: invisible alliances, dual loyalties, role of key functions (HR, legal, finance, operations). Objective: make the governance architecture readable and reconfigure it without uncontrolled breakage.

What for?

  • New leader arriving in a highly political organisation.
  • Board doubts about the reliability of human and social reporting.
  • HR or legal functions perceived as part of the problem.

Format & deliverables

  • HR & governance due diligence (interviews, trace review, synthesis).
  • Mapping of alliances, counter-powers and risk hot spots.
  • Plan to reconfigure arbitration circuits and the role of key functions.

Associated fields:  HR & governance due diligence, Management of invisible alliances, Neutralisation of counter-powers.

Programme 3 – Leadership trajectories & executive narrative (6–9 months)

Support for individual leaders or small executive circles in exposed contexts: contested appointment, past crisis, doubts from the board or teams. Objective: rebuild a trajectory that is credible, legible and assumable.

What for?

  • Leader weakened but considered indispensable.
  • New leadership facing a “legacy organisation”.
  • Need for a strong executive narrative vis-à-vis a board or shareholders.

Format & deliverables

  • Initial perception diagnostic (internal / external, existing traces).
  • Construction of an executive narrative and associated proof points.
  • Ongoing support, including during critical moments.

Associated fields:  Executive repositioning, Narrative dominance & operational AI, Sensitive exits & transitions.

HR & governance due diligence

Before or after a deal, a reorganisation or a leadership change, lay bare the real human architecture: key people, weaknesses, loyalties, social or compliance risks – without triggering internal panic.

  • • Preparing / securing an acquisition or merger
  • • Doubts about the reliability of HR reporting
  • • Need to see beyond org charts

Make loyalties and tacit pacts visible: alumni networks, manager coalitions, HR / operations alliances, interference with external networks. Then recombine these alliances without blowing up the system.

  • • Projects systematically slowed by the same circles

Sensitive exits & transitions

Orchestrate the departure, move or handover of a leader or key profile without destabilising the organisation: exit narrative, timeline, legal and social protections, continuity of authority, role of the successor.

  • • Replacing a leader who has lost trust
  • • Negotiated exit after governance conflict
  • • Transition of a central figure into a group-level role

Neutralisation of counter-powers

Distinguish necessary counter-powers from mechanisms of systematic obstruction, then restructure decision circuits to restore the capacity to act, within an explicit legal and ethical framework.

  • • Support functions turned into control bottlenecks
  • • Works councils or internal coalitions exercising de facto veto
  • • Former leader still weighing on key decisions

Post-crisis resilience

Stabilise after a social conflict, media crisis, compliance investigation or restructuring. Avoid relapse, address invisible after-effects, install safeguards that prevent the system from collapsing in the same way again.

  • • Crises “closed” on paper but still present in people’s minds
  • • Organisation stuck in survival or shock mode
  • • Need to anchor crisis lessons into governance
Structure the rebound

External influence scenarisation

Frame how your sensitive decisions are interpreted by shareholders, media, authorities, partners and ecosystems. Messages, proof points, timing, channels and spokespeople – so that others do not tell your story in your place.

  • • Restructurings, closures, arbitrations under heavy visibility
  • • Ethical, compliance or reputational incidents to explain
  • • Conflicts of vision with a shareholder or influence bloc
Design the offensive

Investment & fee framework

GhostOps intervenes exclusively on situations with high human, financial or reputational stakes. Fees do not follow a classic “consultant day-rate” logic, but an engagement on a defined perimeter, with a high level of requirement in terms of seriousness and operational impact.

1. Initial reading & framing

A targeted diagnostic of the situation (HR crisis, weakened leader, internal power games, influence issues) designed to:

  • assess whether GhostOps is relevant or not;
  • identify real levers and red lines;
  • propose, where relevant, a suitable intervention device.

Depending on the case, this initial reading may be included in the package or billed separately.

2. Fixed-fee intervention sequences

In crisis or tension situations (war-room, neutralisation of blockages, sensitive exit or transition, HR & governance due diligence), fees are defined per sequence (for example 30, 60 or 90 days), with:

  • a clearly bounded perimeter;
  • identified deliverables;
  • an explicit intervention timeline.

3. Trajectory & influence support

For executive repositioning, narrative dominance or external influence, GhostOps operates on the basis of a recurring engagement (retainer) over a minimum period, with a pre-defined volume of interventions and deliverables.

After a confidential brief, you receive:

  • a written confirmation of the relevance (or not) of a GhostOps intervention;
  • a structured proposal (perimeter, timeline, team, fees);
  • payment terms (deposit, milestones, balance, potential outcome-linked component).

Payment is made by bank transfer or via a secure payment link sent after mission validation. No intervention is launched without a clear, written agreement on these elements.

FAQ – Legal, GDPR & confidentiality

Are exchanges with GhostOps truly confidential?

Yes. Information shared in a brief is treated as highly sensitive data. It is only accessible to people who are strictly necessary for the analysis of the case. No document is circulated internally or externally without explicit consent, and information flows are limited to the relevant perimeter.

How does GhostOps handle personal data (GDPR)?

GhostOps interventions often involve complex HR situations. We apply GDPR principles: data minimisation, restriction of recipients, controlled retention periods and secured exchanges. When non-compliance risks are identified on the client side, they are flagged and integrated into the intervention strategy.

Do you intervene on legally sensitive cases?

Yes, but within a clear framework: GhostOps is not a law firm and does not replace legal counsel. We work in coordination with in-house legal teams and external counsel, focusing on the human, political and narrative dimensions of the situation, in line with legal and regulatory constraints.

How are your interventions traced and documented?

The level of documentation depends on how exposed the case is. Some work remains at the stage of orientation notes, others result in opposable elements (memos, scenarios, timelines). In all cases, traceability is controlled to avoid any boomerang effect in the event of litigation, audit or external review.
